Project Workflow: Key Tasks for Phase 6

Task 1 & 2: Audience Analysis and Core Message

Task 1 (Audience): Understand your audience (instructor, peers). They are technical but may not know the specifics of your dataset. Your goal is to convince them that your project is well-executed and your conclusions are valid.

Task 2 (Core Message): What is the single most important takeaway from your project? Define this "thesis statement" first. Every part of your presentation should support this core message.

Task 3 & 4: Structuring Your Presentation and Designing Slides

Task 3 (Structure): A logical structure is key. Use the interactive guide below to plan the sections of your presentation.

Task 5 & 6: Crafting Your Narrative and Preparing for a Demo

Task 5 (Narrative): Don't just list facts. Tell a story: start with the problem, describe the journey of your analysis (the challenges and discoveries), and build towards your final, impactful conclusion.

Task 6 (Live Demo Prep - Optional): If you plan a live demo, prepare meticulously. Have a backup plan (screenshots/video). Test it on the presentation machine if possible. Keep it short and focused on the most impressive part of your project.

Task 7 & 8: Rehearsal and Handling Q&A

Task 7 (Rehearsal): Rehearse your presentation multiple times. Time yourself to ensure you fit within the allotted time. Practice builds confidence and smooths out your delivery.

Task 8 (Q&A Prep): Anticipate potential questions about your methodology, data limitations, and results. Prepare concise answers. It's okay to say, "That's an interesting point, I'd have to look into that further," if you don't know an answer.

Task 9 & 10: Final Pre-Flight Checks

Task 9 (Technical Check): Ensure your presentation file works. If presenting online, test your microphone and camera. Have a backup of your presentation on a USB drive or cloud storage.

Task 10 (Self-Care): Get a good night's sleep before your presentation. A well-rested mind performs better!
