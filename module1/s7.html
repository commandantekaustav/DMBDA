Lab Objectives

Upon completion of this lab session, you will be able to:

  • Apply the phases of the Data Mining Lifecycle (e.g., CRISP-DM) to a given case study or problem scenario.
  • Identify appropriate data mining tasks, potential tools, and techniques relevant to each lifecycle stage for a specific problem.
  • Critically analyze a case study to understand its business objectives, data considerations, and data mining approach.
  • Further develop and refine initial ideas for your mini-project, considering feasibility and the structured lifecycle.
  • Collaborate with peers (if applicable) to brainstorm project components and potential challenges.

Lab Introduction

This lab session builds directly on your understanding of the Data Mining Lifecycle discussed in Session 6. The primary goal is to move from theory to practice by applying the lifecycle framework to concrete examples and your own developing mini-project ideas. You will analyze a case study to see how the lifecycle plays out in a real(istic) scenario and then use this understanding to refine your own project concepts.

Active participation, critical thinking, and discussion (if working in groups) are key to maximizing the learning from this lab.

Lab Activities & Task Breakdown

Your instructor will provide a specific case study for analysis. The following activities will guide you through the lab.

üßêActivity 1: Case Study Analysis & Lifecycle Mapping (Approx. 60 mins)

Thoroughly read and analyze the provided case study. As you do, map the information presented in the case study to the six phases of the CRISP-DM lifecycle (or an alternative model if specified by your instructor).

For each CRISP-DM phase, try to identify or infer from the case study:

  • Business Understanding:
    • What was the primary business problem or objective?
    • What were the success criteria?
  • Data Understanding:
    • What data sources were used or mentioned?
    • What were some initial insights or challenges related to the data?
  • Data Preparation:
    • What data cleaning, transformation, or feature engineering steps were likely performed (even if not explicitly detailed)?
  • Modeling:
    • What type of data mining task was it (e.g., classification, clustering)?
    • Were specific algorithms mentioned or implied?
  • Evaluation:
    • How was the model's performance likely assessed?
    • Did it meet the business objectives?
  • Deployment:
    • How was the solution put into practice or what were the final recommendations?

Tip: Refer back to the detailed notes and interactive diagram from Session 6 regarding the CRISP-DM lifecycle to help structure your analysis.

Tip: Refer to detailed descriptions of each CRISP-DM phase from Session 6.

üí°Activity 2: Mini-Project Brainstorming & Initial Lifecycle Mapping (Approx. 45 mins)

Now, apply the same lifecycle thinking to your own mini-project ideas that you started brainstorming in Lab 1 (Session 3) and further developed in Session 8.

Select one promising project idea (or work with your group on a shared idea) and try to sketch out an initial plan by considering each phase of the CRISP-DM lifecycle:

For your chosen mini-project idea:

  • Business Understanding:
    • Clearly define the problem your project will address. What question are you trying to answer?
    • What would constitute a "successful" outcome for your project? (e.g., a predictive model with a certain accuracy, interesting discovered patterns, a clear report of findings).
  • Data Understanding:
    • What data do you plan to use? Where will you get it? (Be specific: public datasets, APIs, self-collected).
    • What are the key attributes (columns) you anticipate working with?
    • What potential data quality issues do you foresee?
  • Data Preparation (High-Level):
    • What general data cleaning or transformation steps might be necessary? (You don't need to be exhaustive yet).
  • Modeling (High-Level):
    • What type of data mining task (classification, clustering, regression, association) seems most appropriate for your problem?
    • Can you think of one or two potential algorithms you might explore? (It's okay if this is very preliminary).
  • Evaluation (High-Level):
    • How might you determine if your model or findings are good/useful?
  • Deployment (Conceptual):
    • How would your project's results be presented or used? (e.g., a report, a presentation, a simple web visualization).

This is an initial sketch; details will evolve. The goal is to start thinking systematically.

üí¨Activity 3: Discussion & Peer Feedback (Approx. 15 mins, if applicable)

If working in groups or if directed by your instructor, share your case study analysis and your preliminary mini-project lifecycle map. Provide constructive feedback to your peers. Consider:

  • Are the business objectives clear for the mini-project ideas?
  • Are the data sources realistic and accessible?
  • Have any critical steps in the lifecycle been overlooked?
  • Are there any potential challenges that haven't been considered?

Deliverables / Discussion Points for Lab 2

Depending on your instructor's requirements, you may need to:

  • Submit your written analysis of the provided case study, mapped to the CRISP-DM phases.
  • Submit a preliminary lifecycle plan for your chosen mini-project idea.
  • Actively participate in class or group discussions, sharing insights and feedback.

The primary goal is to demonstrate your ability to apply the data mining lifecycle framework thoughtfully.

Key Takeaways for Session 7

  • The Data Mining Lifecycle (e.g., CRISP-DM) provides a practical and robust framework for structuring real-world data mining projects.
  • Applying the lifecycle to case studies helps solidify understanding of each phase's purpose and tasks.
  • Thinking through the lifecycle for your own project ideas early on helps in identifying potential challenges, data needs, and scope.
  • Data mining is an iterative process; initial plans will evolve as you gain more understanding of the data and the problem.
